
Definition:

A polynomial $p \in \mathcal{P}(\mathbb{F})$ is said to have `degree` $m$ if there exists scalars $a_0, a_1, ..., a_m \in \mathbb{F}$ with $a_m \neq 0$ such that for every $z \in \mathbb{F}$ we have
$p(z) = a_0 + a_1 z + ... + a_m z^m$

The polynomial that is identically 0 is said to have degree $-\infty$

The degree of a polynomial p is denoted by $\operatorname{deg} p$
