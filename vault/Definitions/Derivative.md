## Flash Cards

- What is the notation for a differentiable function $f$?
	- The **derivative** of $f$ is $f'$
- Something "having" differentiability is a type of what?
	- differentiability is a **property**
- Memory Jog: whats the derivative of [$]f(x) = x^2[/$]
	- [$]f'(x) = 2x[/$]
- What is the formal definition of derivative? (formal)
	- [$]f'(x) = \operatorname{lim}_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{f(x + h) - f(x)}{h} [/$]
- What is the laymans description of a **derivative**?
	- Derivative = "what the slope is"
- What assumptions can you make about operations on **continuous functions**?
	- the sum of continuous functions is continuous  
	- the scalar multiple of a continuous functions is continuous
	- Source: standard facts of calculus
- What is the laymans description of differentiability?
	- Differentiable = "has a slope"
- What is the relationship between differentiability and continuous functions?
	- Differentiable functions must be continuous  
	- But, not all continous functions are differentiable
	- So, differentiability is a stronger property
- What does a **function** have if its **differentiable**?
	- That the function has a **derivative**
- What "type" is the derivative?
	- An **object/value** when the property of **differentiability** holds
- When we say something has "locally linear behavior" near point a how do we express that formally? F(x) = ?
	- Near a point [$]a[/$]:
	- [$]f(x) \approx f(a) + f'(a)(x-a)[/$] 
	- Making smooth functions look locally linear
