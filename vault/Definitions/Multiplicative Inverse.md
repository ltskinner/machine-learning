
## Definition

For every $\alpha \neq 0$, there exists a unique $\beta$ such that:

$\alpha\beta = 1$

## Intuition

Every nonzero element can be **undone** multiplicatively

### Why it Matters

Enables division and defines fields

### Failure Case

$\mathbb{Z}$ (integers) lack multiplicative inverses (are not a field)
