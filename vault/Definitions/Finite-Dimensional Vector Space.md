
A [[Vector Space]] is called `finite-dimensional` if some list of vectors in it [[Span]]s the space


A vector space is called `infinite-dimensional` if it is **NOT** `finite-dimensional`


## Flash Cards

- What makes a vector space **finite-dimensional**
	- A **vector space** is called **finite-dimensional** if:
	- It contains some **list of vectors** that **spans** the vector space
- What does being a **finite-dimensional vector space** guarantee?
	- Having a basis
- What is the key structural difference between finite- and infinite-dimensional vector spaces?
	- Finite-dimensional: there is a **maximum length** of **linearly independent lists**  
	- Infinite-dimensional: no max size - extend indefinitely
- What is the relation between a finite-dimensional vector space and its **subspaces**
	- Every **subspace** of a **finite-dimensional vector space** is finite-dimensional
- What is an _in_**finite-dimensional** vector space?
	- A vector space that is **NOT** finite-dimensional
